McKinley Johnson, Playwright

About McKinley Johnson

McKinley Johnson is an artistic associate of the Open Door Repertory where his past credits include the world premiere of his musical, Clara(2013 Black Theatre Alliance Award/Best Musical, nominated for Best Direction) and Eye of the Storm: The Bayard Rustin Story(2009 Nominated for Best Musical and Best Direction). In 2012 at Open Door, Mr Johnson directed his heartfelt musical, Train is Comin': The Story of the Fisk Jubilee in the 1870s. This is the sixth production of Train, which was first produced at Chicago Theatre Company. While at Open Door he has also directed Crowns and Smokie Joe's Cafe. Mr Johnson's musicals Being Beautiful and Georgia Tomproduced at Bailiwick Repertory garnered and After Dark Award and Jeff Nominations. He was commissioned by the Steppenwolf to compose and arrange spirituals for Harriet Jacobs by Lydia Diamond. Mr. Johnson's musical The Nativity, produced by Congo Square Theatre, seen at the Goodman Theatre and produced for four years was called "warm and enjoyable." He is the recipient of several Pilgrim Project Grants, National Endowment for the Arts/Theatre Communications Group Grant, and Black History Commission/Touchstone Theatre. He is a full-time faculty member of Dominican University and the adjunct for International Academy of Design and Technology.

Eye of the Storm

Eye Of The Storm

This musical poignantly delves into the fractious meetings of the burgeoning civil rights movement and the influence of political activist Bayard Rustin. With over twenty-five original songs flavored in the traditions of gospel, blues, spirituals, opera and musical theatre, Eye of the Storm challenges and agitates the history of Civil Rights brought to America through the life of Mr. Rustin.

Clara

Clara

Driven by her mother's reckless ambition, Clara, inspired by true events in the life of Clara Ward Singers, is a musical that finds a talented young musician trapped between her dream to be a wife/mother and her mother Gertrude's goal to make her a gospel legend.

It Don't Just ...Shake Off

It Don't Just ...Shake Off

Set in the 1920s, this musical is based on the true partnership of bluesmen, Tampa Red - the Guitar Wizard (Hudson Whittaker) ang Georgia Tom (Thomas Dorsey - the Father of Gospel Music, composer of "Precious Lord, Take My Hand").

Being Beautiful

Being Beautiful

Being Beautiful is a sensitive story of lifestyle choices. It follows the journey of a young man named Afton Cousins. After fleeing to the the city of Chicago, Afton meets Ms. Lonette, a show club owner, mentor and female impersonator who teaches him some meaningful lessons, the kind that speak to pride, courage, love of family and being true to one's self.

Train Is Comin'

Train Is Comin'

Set in the 1800's, Train Is Comin'is a musical play of faith, courage, self-discovery, the inhumanity of man to man, and the emergence of the Negro Spirituals in American culture.

Georgia Tom

Georgia Tom

Georgia Tom, is a musical based on the life of Thomas Andrew Dorsey - The Father of Gospel Music.
